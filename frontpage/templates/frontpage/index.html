Who Am I?

Hello Internet! I'm Nate Jones, a Computer Science student at the University of Michigan.

If you have made it here, then you are probably wondering who I am, and what I do. Well here's my background:

Originally I was a high school student at Cedar Springs High School in West Michigan. Most of my time was spent either with the high school band or with the track team. I enjoyed messing with technology, but didn't start programming until I got into college.

Once I got into college, I picked up programming pretty quickly, and was lucky enough to join an awesome project called Whisper my freshman year, which is working on creating applications and technology that circumvents censorship. As I was still relatively new to programming, most of my work was fixing simple bugs and creating tools for other developers.

Unfortunately due to personal money and time constraints, I had to leave the project at the end of the summer of 2013. I currently work IT at the University.


I'm still figuring out what my interests are in the Computer Science world, so I'm learning about many different fields to see what I want to focus on in school.

Most of my current experience is with C++ (what most CS classes here are taught in). I worked with Java (and Android) while I worked with the Whisper project. I built this website in my free time using Python and Django. Projects and such will be hosted here eventually.

If you're looking for my resume, there is a link at the top of the page.

If you have any other questions, you can shoot me an email at jonesnl@umich.edu

Thanks! Nate
